Questions researchers ask

Everything you need to know before joining

Is SwiftsReader actually designed for ADHD — or is that just marketing? ▼
Every feature is traceable to peer-reviewed research. RSVP reading is backed by a 2025 PubMed study showing +13% comprehension in ADHD readers. Break reminders are grounded in Biwer et al. (2023) on Pomodoro-style breaks. Dyslexia mode implements the British Dyslexia Association's evidence-based typography guidelines. We publish our full research bibliography in the app.
Does it work on mobile? My traffic comes from my phone. ▼
Yes — SwiftsReader is a Progressive Web App (PWA) built mobile-first. 80% of our current users are on mobile (primarily Android in India). Install it from your browser home screen for a native app experience with no App Store needed. The RSVP reader, AI summaries, and dyslexia mode all work fully on mobile.
What's the difference between the Free and Pro plan? ▼
Free gives you 20 AI summaries per month and up to 20 papers stored — enough to use SwiftsReader daily as a researcher. Pro adds unlimited papers, unlimited AI summaries, cloud sync across devices, and priority support. No credit card is required for the free plan — just sign up and start reading.
How does the AI summarisation work? Is my paper data private? ▼
Summaries are generated using Claude (Anthropic's AI) via a secure backend — your paper content is never stored on our servers beyond the session, and never used to train AI models. We process section-by-section so you get contextual summaries, not a generic abstract rewrite. Privacy policy will be published at launch.
Is SwiftsReader available now, or is it still in beta? ▼
SwiftsReader is live now — you can open the app immediately with a free account. No waitlist, no credit card. The free plan gives you 20 AI summaries per month and up to 20 papers. Pro (unlimited summaries, cloud sync, priority support) is $12/month or $6/month for students with a .edu email.
How does the audio reader work? Is it robotic text-to-speech? ▼
No — SwiftsReader uses OpenAI's TTS (Nova voice) which sounds genuinely natural, not robotic. As the audio plays, each word is highlighted in sync so you can follow along visually. This dual-channel input (hearing + seeing) is particularly effective for dyslexic readers and for absorbing dense academic content. You can listen hands-free while commuting or walking, or use audio alongside the RSVP reader.
I'm a researcher in India. Is pricing in INR? ▼
INR pricing and UPI payment support is coming at launch — we know 76% of our waitlist is based in India and we're building for that audience specifically. The free tier has no payment required at all, and Pro will be priced at a rate accessible to Indian researchers and students.

Ready to read smarter?

The Free plan lets you try SwiftsReader with up to 3 papers and 5 AI summaries per month. The Student plan ($7.99/mo) gives you 50 papers, unlimited summaries, and HD Voice. The Pro plan ($15/mo) includes everything unlimited plus full PDF extraction — figures, tables, and equations are automatically detected and displayed using our academic PDF engine, making it ideal for researchers working with complex scientific papers from publishers like Elsevier, Springer, and Sage.
